The One-Sentence Answer

Bosch is the specialist. Their 12V drills are best-in-class for compact ergonomics. They make the best drill for apartment living. Makita is the generalist. Their 18V ecosystem is deeper, their tools are more reliable over decades, and their manufacturing quality is legendary. For most people buying into a long-term platform: Makita. For a compact drill that fits small hands perfectly: Bosch.

Where Bosch Wins

12V leadership. Bosch's 12V platform is the most refined compact tool system available. The GSR12V-300 is our top pick for apartment living, small hands, and anyone who prioritizes weight over raw power. The 12V Flexiclick system adds interchangeable chucks — drill, right-angle, offset, hex — that are genuinely useful for cabinet installers and electricians working in tight spaces.

Better for small hands. Across the board, Bosch grips are slimmer and triggers have shorter reach than Makita equivalents. For users with smaller hands, this difference is meaningful — a Bosch drill feels like an extension of your hand. A Makita drill feels like a tool you're holding.

Innovation in accessories. The Flexiclick interchangeable chuck system, the wireless charging L-BOXX system, and the integrated Bluetooth tool tracking in their professional line show Bosch is thinking beyond just making a drill spin.

Where Makita Wins

21 years of battery backward compatibility. A Makita battery from 2005 works in a 2026 Makita tool. Bosch has changed battery interfaces multiple times. If you're buying tools to own for 15+ years, Makita's commitment to backward compatibility is the single strongest argument for the brand.

Longevity. Independent professional surveys consistently show Makita tools last the longest without repairs. The company manufactures their own motors, electronics, and many of their own battery cells — unusual vertical integration that gives them end-to-end quality control. Bosch tools are well-made, but Makita tools are overbuilt.

Ecosystem depth. Makita's 18V LXT system has 200+ tools. Bosch's 18V system has about 60. If you want to buy one battery platform and use it across every tool you'll ever own — including outdoor power equipment — Makita has the deeper catalog.

For the Homeowner

If you're hanging shelves, assembling furniture, and hanging curtain rods: the Bosch GSR12V-300 is the best drill for this life. It's lighter than any Makita, the grip fits smaller hands better, and the $99 kit includes two batteries and a case. Makita's equivalent 12V drill costs $10 more and weighs 0.3 lbs more. The weight difference sounds small on paper — after holding it overhead for two minutes while your other hand positions a shelf bracket, you'll feel every ounce.

If you see yourself expanding to more than just a drill — circular saw, sander, outdoor equipment — go Makita from the start. The Bosch 12V ecosystem is limited. The Makita 18V LXT ecosystem is the safest long-term bet in the entire tool industry. Start with the XFD13 drill at $129 and you'll never need to replace it.

The Bottom Line

Buy Bosch if you want the best compact 12V drill for apartment living. The GSR12V-300 is unbeatable at its size, weight, and price. Buy Makita if you see yourself owning more than one cordless tool. The 18V ecosystem is deeper, the tools last longer, and the 21-year battery compatibility means your investment is safer. For a single drill you'll use four times a year: Bosch. For a drill that anchors a growing tool collection: Makita.
